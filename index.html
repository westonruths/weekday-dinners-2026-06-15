Monday, June 15

Lemon–green garlic chicken thighs, pearl couscous, and sautéed CSA greens

Protein: chicken thighs · Carb: pearl couscous · Veg: spinach/beet or turnip greens + green garlic/scapes · Time: 40–45 min

uses 1.25 lb chicken packuses delicate greens firsttoddler-friendly

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1½ cups pearl couscous
  • 2¼ cups low-sodium chicken broth or water
  • 6–8 cups CSA spinach, beet greens, turnip greens, or a mix, washed and chopped
  • 2 green garlic stalks or garlic scapes, thinly sliced; use scallions if needed
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 1 tsp paprika or smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp dried oregano or thyme
  • Salt and pepper; keep Aria’s portion lighter on salt

Steps

  1. Pat chicken dry. Season with paprika, oregano, pepper, lemon zest, and a moderate pinch of salt.
  2. Start couscous: toast it in 1 tbsp olive oil for 2 minutes, add broth/water, cover, and simmer 10–12 minutes until tender. Rest covered.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet. Sear chicken 5–6 minutes per side until browned and cooked through to 165°F. Move to a plate.
  4. In the same pan, sauté green garlic/scapes 1 minute. Add greens by handfuls with a splash of water. Cook 3–5 minutes until wilted and tender.
  5. Finish greens with lemon juice and a drizzle of olive oil. Slice chicken and serve over couscous with greens on the side or folded in.

Aria serving: chop chicken small; mix couscous with a little olive oil/lemon; finely chop cooked greens.

Tuesday, June 16

Skin-on sockeye salmon, quinoa, and roasted broccoli–kale

Protein: sockeye salmon · Carb: quinoa · Veg: older broccoli + chopped kale · Time: 35–45 min

uses older broccoli/chopped kalefish nightminimal cleanup

Ingredients

  • 1.25–1.5 lb Alaskan sockeye skin-on fillets
  • 1¼ cups quinoa, rinsed
  • 2½ cups water or broth
  • 1 large head broccoli or 12–16 oz florets
  • 4 cups chopped kale
  • 1 lemon, half sliced and half juiced
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, grated or minced
  • Salt, pepper, and optional garlic powder

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Start quinoa with water/broth; simmer covered about 15 minutes, then rest 5 minutes.
  2. Toss broccoli with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic. Roast on a sheet pan 12 minutes.
  3. Add chopped kale to the sheet pan with a small drizzle of oil. Toss and push vegetables to one side.
  4. Pat salmon dry. Season with pepper, a little salt, lemon juice, and optional garlic powder. Add to the sheet pan skin-side down with lemon slices on top.
  5. Roast 8–12 minutes, depending on thickness, until salmon flakes and reaches your preferred doneness.
  6. Serve salmon with quinoa and broccoli–kale; squeeze lemon over the adult plates.

Aria serving: check carefully for bones, remove skin if tough, and flake salmon into small pieces.

Wednesday, June 17

Soy–sesame ground pork lettuce rice bowls

Protein: ground pork · Carb: sushi rice · Veg: butter/green/purple lettuce + cilantro/scallions · Time: 30–40 min

uses lettuce volumeuses cilantro/scallionsdeconstructed for toddler

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1½ cups uncooked sushi rice
  • 1–2 heads CSA butter/green/purple lettuce, washed and dried
  • 3 scallions, sliced
  • ½ cup cilantro, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger, or ½ tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar or lemon/lime juice
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Optional adults-only: chili crisp, sriracha, or gochujang at the table

Steps

  1. Start sushi rice in the rice cooker or on the stove.
  2. Brown pork in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up well, 6–8 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and the white/light-green scallion parts. Cook 1 minute.
  4. Stir in soy sauce, vinegar/lemon, and sesame oil. Simmer 1–2 minutes until glossy.
  5. Chop some lettuce for bowls and leave some leaves whole for wraps. Toss lettuce with cilantro, remaining scallions, a squeeze of lemon/lime, and a tiny drizzle of oil.
  6. Serve rice topped with pork and lettuce salad; adults can add chili at the table.

Aria serving: rice + pork with very finely chopped lettuce or a cooked green on the side; skip chili and go light on salty sauce.

Thursday, June 18

Shrimp fried rice with spinach, scallions, and eggs

Protein: shrimp + eggs · Carb: sushi rice · Veg: spinach/scallions/cilantro · Time: 25–35 min with cooked rice; under 60 with fresh rice

uses raw shrimpuses leftover ricefastest dinner

Ingredients

  • 1 lb raw shrimp, peeled/deveined if needed
  • 4–5 cups cooked sushi rice, ideally chilled or at least cooled
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 4 cups spinach or mixed delicate greens, chopped
  • 3 scallions, sliced
  • ½ cup cilantro, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1–2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1½–2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Optional: frozen peas/carrots if you have them

Steps

  1. If rice is not already cooked, start it first and spread it on a tray for a few minutes so it steams off.
  2. Pat shrimp dry. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet; cook shrimp 1–2 minutes per side until pink. Remove and chop a few pieces smaller for Aria.
  3. Add a little more oil if needed. Scramble eggs quickly, then move them to the shrimp plate.
  4. Cook garlic and scallion whites 30 seconds. Add rice and stir-fry 3–5 minutes, breaking up clumps.
  5. Add spinach/greens and cook until wilted. Return shrimp and eggs. Season with soy sauce and sesame oil.
  6. Finish with scallion greens and cilantro.

Aria serving: cut shrimp into small pieces lengthwise/crosswise, and keep her portion lower sodium.

Pork belly lettuce wraps with rice and sangchu-geotjeori-style salad

Protein: pork belly slices · Carb: sushi rice · Veg: CSA lettuce + scallions/cilantro · Time: 35–45 min if pork is thawed

more fun Friday dinneruses lettuce hardthaw Thursday night
For the lettuce salad baseline, use the household standard: Maangchi’s sangchu-geotjeori. The version below keeps Aria’s portion unspiced/less salty and lets adults add heat separately.

Ingredients

  • 1–1.25 lb frozen pork belly slices, thawed overnight
  • 1½ cups uncooked sushi rice
  • 2 heads or several big handfuls CSA lettuce leaves, washed and dried
  • 3 scallions, sliced
  • ½ cup cilantro, optional but useful if still fresh
  • 1 cucumber, optional buy, cut into sticks
  • 1 lemon or 1–2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, optional
  • Adults-only add-ons: gochujang, ssamjang, kimchi, chili crisp, or gochugaru

Steps

  1. Start sushi rice. While it cooks, wash and dry lettuce thoroughly.
  2. For a mild family lettuce salad, toss chopped lettuce with scallions, cilantro, lemon/rice vinegar, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sesame seeds. Pull out Aria’s portion before adding adult heat.
  3. Heat a cast-iron pan or griddle over medium-high. Cook pork belly slices in batches until browned and cooked through, 2–4 minutes per side depending on thickness.
  4. Drain pork on a towel-lined plate. Cut into bite-size pieces.
  5. Serve as wraps: lettuce leaf + rice + pork + cucumber + adult sauce. Or serve deconstructed bowls for easier toddler eating.

Aria serving: choose leaner, softer pork pieces, cut very small; serve rice and mild lettuce/cucumber separately.

Small grocery list

  • Optional toddler sides: avocado, berries/fruit, plain yogurt, or cottage cheese.
  • Fresh/frozen white fish only if the already-thawed mahi mahi is not used Sunday and needs replacing.
